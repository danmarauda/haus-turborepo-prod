# First Home Buyer Guide - South Australia (SA)

## Overview

South Australia offers strong support for first home buyers with a $15,000 grant, stamp duty relief, and HomeStart Finance's innovative loan products including the Graduate Loan.

## First Home Owner Grant (FHOG)

### Grant Amount
- **$15,000** for new homes
- Property value must be **$650,000 or less**

### Eligibility Requirements
1. Australian citizen or permanent resident
2. At least 18 years old
3. Never owned property in Australia (you or spouse)
4. Must live in the property for 6 continuous months
5. Must move in within 12 months of completion

## Stamp Duty Relief

### First Home Buyer Relief
| Property Value | Relief |
|----------------|--------|
| Up to $650,000 | Full exemption on new homes |
| Existing homes | No specific FHB exemption (standard rates) |

### Standard Stamp Duty Rates (Existing Homes)
| Purchase Price | Stamp Duty |
|----------------|------------|
| $400,000 | ~$16,330 |
| $500,000 | ~$21,330 |
| $600,000 | ~$26,830 |

**Note:** SA doesn't offer stamp duty concessions for existing homes, making new builds more attractive for first home buyers.

## HomeStart Finance

### What is HomeStart?
South Australia's government-backed home loan provider offering flexible options for first home buyers.

### Loan Products

#### Graduate Loan
- For recent university graduates
- Borrow up to 98% of property value
- No LMI required
- Competitive rates

#### Low Deposit Loan
- Deposit as low as 3%
- No LMI
- Income limits apply

#### Shared Equity Option
- HomeStart contributes up to 25%
- Lower repayments
- Buy out their share over time

### Eligibility
- Income limits vary by product
- Generally under $90,000 (single) or $120,000 (couple)
- Must be SA resident

## Step-by-Step Journey

### 1. Maximize Benefits
- [ ] Target new homes under $650,000 for $15,000 grant + stamp duty exemption
- [ ] Consider HomeStart if you have limited deposit
- [ ] Check Graduate Loan if recently graduated

### 2. Financial Preparation
- [ ] Save 3-5% deposit (HomeStart) or 10-20% (standard)
- [ ] Get pre-approval

- [ ] Stamp duty exemption: Applied at settlement
