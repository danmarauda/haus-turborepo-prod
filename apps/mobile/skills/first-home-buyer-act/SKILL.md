# First Home Buyer Guide - Australian Capital Territory (ACT)

## Overview

The ACT offers the **Home Buyer Concession Scheme** with significant stamp duty savings. Note that the ACT does not offer a First Home Owner Grant, but the stamp duty concessions are substantial.

## First Home Owner Grant

**Important:** The ACT does **not** currently offer a First Home Owner Grant. However, the Home Buyer Concession Scheme provides significant stamp duty relief.

## Home Buyer Concession Scheme

### Eligibility Requirements
1. Australian citizen or permanent resident
2. At least 18 years old
3. Total household income under **$170,000** (2024-25)
4. Never received the concession before
5. Must live in the property for at least 1 year
6. Property value up to **$1,000,000**

### Stamp Duty Savings
| Property Value | Standard Duty | With Concession | Savings |
|----------------|---------------|-----------------|---------|
| $500,000 | $11,400 | $0 | $11,400 |
| $600,000 | $14,280 | $0 | $14,280 |
| $700,000 | $17,765 | $0 | $17,765 |
| $800,000 | $23,810 | $0 | $23,810 |
| $900,000 | $30,660 | ~$7,665 | ~$22,995 |

### Key Points
- Full exemption for properties up to approximately $800,000
- Sliding scale reduction up to $1,000,000
- Applies to both new and established homes
- Income threshold is gross household income

## Pensioner Duty Concession

If you're a pensioner buying your first home:
- Additional concessions available
- Can be combined with other schemes
- Contact ACT Revenue for details

## Step-by-Step Journey

### 1. Check Eligibility
- [ ] Confirm household income under $170,000
- [ ] Verify you haven't received the concession before
- [ ] Ensure you can live in property for 1+ year

### 2. Financial Preparation
- [ ] Save 10-20% deposit (no FHOG to assist)
- [ ] Get pre-approval from a lender
- [ ] Budget for other purchase costs

### 3. Property Search
- [ ] Use HAUS to find properties under $800,000 for full exemption
- [ ] Consider Canberra's outer suburbs for value
- [ ] Both new and established homes qualify

### 4. Apply for Concession
- [ ] Apply through ACT Revenue Office
- [ ] Provide income evidence (tax returns, payslips)
- [ ] Submit before or at settlement

## Comparison: ACT vs Other States

| State | FHOG (New) | Stamp Duty Relief |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| **ACT** | $0 | Up to ~$24,000 |
| NSW | $10,000 | Up to ~$31,000 |
| VIC | $10,000 | Up to ~$31,000 |
| QLD | $30,000 | Up to ~$16,000 |

While ACT lacks a FHOG, the stamp duty concession is competitive, especially for higher-value properties.
