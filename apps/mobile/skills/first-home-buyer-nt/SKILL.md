# First Home Buyer Guide - Northern Territory (NT)

## Overview

The Northern Territory offers affordable housing, the First Home Owner Grant, stamp duty concessions, and the unique HomeBuild Access scheme for building new homes.

## First Home Owner Grant (FHOG)

### Grant Amount
- **$10,000** for new homes
- No property value cap (unlike other states!)

### Eligibility Requirements
1. Australian citizen or permanent resident
2. At least 18 years old
3. Never owned property in Australia (you or spouse)
4. Must live in the property for 6 continuous months
5. Must move in within 12 months of completion

## Stamp Duty Concessions

### First Home Owner Discount
| Property Value | Discount |
|----------------|----------|
| Up to $650,000 | Full exemption ($0 duty) |
| Over $650,000 | Standard rates apply |

### Savings Example
| Purchase Price | Standard Duty | First Home Buyer | Savings |
|----------------|---------------|------------------|---------|
| $400,000 | $18,697 | $0 | $18,697 |
| $500,000 | $23,928 | $0 | $23,928 |
| $600,000 | $29,428 | $0 | $29,428 |

**Note:** NT has some of the highest standard stamp duty rates, making the first home buyer exemption extremely valuable!

## HomeBuild Access

### What is HomeBuild Access?
A NT Government scheme providing grants for building new homes.

### Grant Amounts
- **$10,000** for building a new home
- Can be combined with FHOG for **$20,000 total**

### Eligibility
- Building a new home in the NT
- Property value limits may apply
- Must be principal place of residence

## BuildBonus Grant

### Additional Support
- Extra grants may be available for building
- Check current programs with NT Government
- Can stack with other benefits

## Step-by-Step Journey

### 1. Maximize Benefits
- [ ] Target new builds for FHOG + HomeBuild Access ($20,000)
- [ ] Stay under $650,000 for full stamp duty exemption
- [ ] Consider building vs buying established
